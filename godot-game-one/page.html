Mystery Story-Rich Supernatural Investigation Science-Fiction

Welcome to your new role in the claims department at Bliss life insurance. Investigate client deaths by searching for objects from the scene. Move them around in 3D to find hidden details and unlock new names. Collect more objects, details, and names to piece together the true cause of death—and the true nature of the company. Leave no stone unturned in this story-rich supernatural mystery.

Release: 2026 Platform: Steam

STORY

Concept art by Hella Petersen

Glasgow, 1968

A job's a job, right? Well this one’s properly weird. Working in cubicled isolation, you rarely see your fellow cogs in the company machine. Never mind its aloof founder, the venerable Mr. Bliss. You begin to notice strange anomalies in your collection of objects: a letter opener that looks like a ritual knife, a paperweight in the shape of a disturbing entity. And now your curious cubicle neighbours have roped you into a secret side investigation.

GAMEPLAY

Each case in Death Insurance has an information pack with everything you need to prove the client's true cause of death.

  • A report describing the scene.
  • A sketch of the scene.
  • A memory-card with a scan of the scene.

Concept art by Hella Petersen

There's a 3D model for every object in the scene, but each object must be unlocked first. Reading the report and checking the sketch will give the player prompts to type into the search bar. As you get closer to a match, the swirling display will coalesce into that object.

3D models in the 1960s? It's not so far-fetched in this story, where the development of the film camera was stunted and the personal computer catapulted forward by an emerging science, Lexicontology.

Ontological cameras (like the one installed inside every client) can capture a scan of everything in a small area. Then a Lexicontological computer queries this unindexed, analogue data, with a word. If an Object responds to the query, its frequency is isolated and a binary representation extracted.

An Ontological Object contains everything that makes say, an apple an apple. So displaying its shape, texture, and shade on a monitor is as easy as apple pie.

Switch back and forth between previously unlocked objects and details as you build up the bigger picture. To crack the case, fill in the perpetrator's first and last name, and select one of your unlocked objects as the instrument of death.

OTTO

Daniel Otto Jack Petersen II

Otto is a Glaswegian programmer and a jack-of-all-trades creator with an MEng in Computer and Electronic Systems. He's worked as a corporate web-developer for six years, specialising in design, digital accessibility, and project management. His side projects have run the gamut from YouTube and Twitch channels, to 3D printing and organisational tools.

Otto has an (un)healthy reverence for the medium, with thousands of hours clocked across every genre under the sun, and has followed the industry closely since the days of the Wii News Channel. In 2025 he entered the GMTK Game Jam with some friends, and created BLEEP, scoring top 1% in the audio category. DAN

Daniel Otto Jack Petersen

Dan is a writer from the U.S., living in Glasgow for the past 25 years. He's a fan of metal, punk, horror, science fiction, Mexican food, and long walks with his dog August.

His short stories have appeared in Hybrid Moments: A Literary Tribute to the Misfits (2016, Weirdpunk Books), Disease of Finitude: David Cronenberg Inspired Microfiction (2025, Antilogos Press), and Folkloric: A Magazine of New Folklore (2025). He has also published numerous academic chapters on contemporary horror fiction. He gained his PhD in English Literature from the University of Glasgow, focused on ‘ecomonstrous poetics’ in R. A. Lafferty and Cormac McCarthy.
